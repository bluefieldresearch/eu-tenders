## Key observations

- **France dominates** with 9 of the top 20 contracts, reflecting an active concession renewal cycle for water and sanitation services.
- **SUEZ Eau France** and **VEOLIA** are the dominant awardees, appearing in 8 of the 20 contracts combined.
- Contracts #1–3, #6, #7, #9 and #10 are **mixed-scope** (construction frameworks or civil works where water/sewage is a significant component but not the sole purpose).
- Contracts #4, #5, #8, #11–20 are **primarily water/wastewater** in scope.
- There is a notable **value gap** between #17 (€95M) and #18 (€21M), suggesting the European water market in Q1 2026 concentrated its largest awards in France, Poland and Czechia.

## Methodology

Queried TED API v3 (`POST /v3/notices/search`) with water-related CPV code prefixes. Results filtered to contract award notices with non-zero values and named awardees. False positives (district heating, gas pipelines, road-only contracts) manually excluded. Non-EUR values converted using ECB reference rates for 24 March 2026.
